# Caleb Stauss — LinkedIn About Section

---

Most teams arrive with a clear sense of what needs to be solved. That certainty is often the first thing worth questioning.

I'm a product designer and strategist with more than fifteen years of experience helping teams turn complex, ambiguous challenges into digital experiences people understand, use, and trust. I work at the intersection of product strategy and design craft — moving between executive conversations and implementation detail without losing context in either direction.

The best product work rarely begins with an interface. It begins with a sharper question: what is the team actually trying to solve, and what assumptions are shaping the answer? I'm often brought in when the path forward is still forming — when a product needs structure, a system has outgrown its foundation, or a new idea has potential but not yet enough shape to build from.

That is where I do my best work.

Over the course of my career I've worked across startups, enterprise platforms, internal tools, operational workflows, Web3 infrastructure, sports media, aviation interfaces, and new product concepts. The industries change. The problem usually doesn't. Unclear complexity and inherited assumptions send teams in the wrong direction before a single decision gets made — my strongest value comes from finding that gap early and building systems that help teams move faster without sacrificing quality.

I'm equally comfortable discussing growth strategy, conversion, and business metrics as I am refining spacing systems, component behavior, and interaction detail. Strong products are never only strategic or only visual — they are the result of thousands of connected decisions, from the business model behind the experience to the smallest moment a user taps, scans, submits, or decides to keep going.

A good screen solves the moment in front of the user. A good system helps the team solve the next hundred moments without starting over.

Based in Northwest Arkansas. Open to product design and design leadership roles where design is treated as a strategic force, not a final layer.
