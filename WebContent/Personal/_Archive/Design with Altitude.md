# Design with Altitude

For over 15 years, I've operated at the intersection of product strategy and design engineering. From concept to launch, scale, and sunset. Strategically grounded enough to shape what gets built. Technically fluent enough to build it myself.

I operate as the thread from first sketch to shipped platform, the constant that keeps vision intact as products evolve and conditions change. Most design problems aren't design problems. They're structure problems: eroding logic, implicit decisions, and system failures that surface as UX friction.

Identifying and defining those symptoms clearly requires new perspective, evolving workflows, and a change of altitude. I use AI-augmentation strategically as a force multiplier, reducing complexity, building momentum, and shipping systems designed for scale.

You can't claim perspective if you never ascend. I've earned mine by designing, building, and launching the things I architect.
