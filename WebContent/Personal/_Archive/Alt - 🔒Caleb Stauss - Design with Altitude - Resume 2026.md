# Design with Altitude

For nearly 20 years, I’ve operated at the intersection of product strategy and design engineering—from defining national product direction for Fortune 500s to building the relational backbones for professional sports leagues and avionics interfaces.

Most design problems aren’t design problems. They are structure problems. Logic gaps. Decisions that were never made explicit. What looks like a UX friction is usually an architecture flaw wearing a UI costume—and the only way to see that clearly is to get above it first.

That’s where I start: high enough to see the whole system, but specific enough to build the logic that makes it work. I don’t just solve problems; I interrogate the assumptions they were built on. I break down the architecture, anticipate the side effects, and dig until the root issue surfaces.

I leverage a deep technical fluency and AI-augmented workflows to ship at a pace that traditional teams can’t match. Because in high-growth environments, the handoff between "vision" and "build" is where the best ideas die. I eliminate the handoff by designing with the precision of an engineer and the perspective of a strategist.

You can’t claim perspective you haven’t earned. I’ve earned mine through years of designing, building and launching the things I architect.
