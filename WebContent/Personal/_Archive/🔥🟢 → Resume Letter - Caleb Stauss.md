# Cover Letter

**To the PayPal Design and Technology Leadership Team,**

When I first started in this industry, the title "App Designer" didn't even exist. As technology shifted, we evolved into UX Designers, and for the past decade, we have been Product Designers. Today, we are standing squarely at the threshold of the next great evolution in what it means to design digital products. 

Right now, the industry is deeply and emotionally invested in our current tooling. Designers love the static canvas, and for many, the thought of moving away from drawing interfaces toward manipulating live, AI-enabled systems is intimidating. This transition isn't going to happen overnight, but the shift is undeniably underway. The gap between static design documentation and live technical implementation is finally collapsing, and I find that incredibly exciting. 

That is why PayPal’s vision for an Experience Intelligence Hub stands out. You aren’t just looking for a faster way to generate mockups; you are actively laying the groundwork for whatever the *next* generation of product design looks like. 

I want to help build that frontier. Over my career, my work has always lived in the space where design intent meets technical reality. Whether I was architecting modular in-store digital infrastructure for Fortune 1 retailers like Walmart, or designing 0→1 secure cryptographic logic for TokenProof (recently acquired by Yuga Labs), the goal has always been to create systems that empower teams to scale safely without restricting them. 

My current independent prototyping is focused entirely on this new horizon. I am exploring how AI-native workflows can act as an asynchronous bridge—observing project memory and translating design intent into live systems. The goal isn't to force designers to become engineers; it is to build intelligent connective tissue that allows them to step into this new phase of design with confidence. 

The next era of design is being built right now, and the hardest problems to solve will be human, not just technical. I would love to share the architectural thinking behind my current prototypes and discuss how we can build an Experience Intelligence Hub at PayPal that brings teams into the future. 

Thank you for your time,

**Caleb Stauss**
