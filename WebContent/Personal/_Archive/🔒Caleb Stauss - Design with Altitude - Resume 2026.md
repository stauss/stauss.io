## Design with Altitude

Most design problems aren't design problems. They're structure problems. Logic gaps. Decisions that were never made explicit. What looks like a UX problem is often an architecture problem wearing a UX costume — and the only way to see that clearly is to get above it first.

That's where I start: high enough to see the whole system, specific enough to find which level the real problem lives on. I don’t just solve problems — I interrogate the assumptions they were built on. I break down the architecture, anticipate the side effects, and dig until the root issue surfaces.

For over 15 years I've worked across every altitude — from founders with a napkin sketch to Fortune 500 enterprise platforms, early-stage startups to live sports infrastructure and avionics interfaces. The range isn't a résumé flex. It's what keeps the perspective honest. You can't claim perspective you haven't earned.
