## Recommended Version

### I help unclear ideas become useful systems.

I’m a product designer, strategist, and builder based in Northwest Arkansas. My best work usually starts before the thing is fully defined, when the idea has energy, the workflow is tangled, or the system has grown faster than the team can explain it.

I don’t start by asking what the screen should look like. I start by asking what has to be true underneath it. What is the model? What are the rules? Where does the work break down? What is manual today that should become part of the system? What decision has everyone been avoiding?

That is the work I’m built for.

For more than 15 years, I’ve worked across enterprise platforms, internal tools, sports infrastructure, Web3 verification, avionics interfaces, healthcare, local business operations, design systems, and early-stage product concepts. The industries change. The pattern usually doesn’t: someone knows something needs to exist, but not what shape it should take yet.

I help find that shape.

Sometimes that becomes a product interface. Sometimes it becomes a workflow, a data model, a prototype, a design system, a technical spec, or the first clear decision a team can actually build from. The surface matters, but the surface is rarely the starting point.

My range is practical. I can move from strategy to Figma, from brand voice to system logic, from stakeholder conversation to frontend implementation, from messy notes to something a team can act on. I care about taste, but not as decoration. Good design should create momentum. It should help people understand what matters and move forward with less friction.

I’m locally grounded, not locally limited. I care about real businesses, real workflows, real users, and tools people can actually use while busy. I also care about high craft, sharp storytelling, strong systems, and work that holds up beside the best product teams and studios anywhere.

Give me the half-formed idea, the inherited system, the messy workflow, or the ambitious product bet that hasn’t found its shape yet.

That’s where the path forward gets designed.
